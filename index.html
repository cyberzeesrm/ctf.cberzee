About the Mission

What is RAD 2.0?

A flagship cybersecurity Capture The Flag competition — where hackers, defenders, and interstellar thinkers collide.

🛰️
The Event

RAD Cyber CTF — Root Access Denied 2.0 is a Jeopardy-style cybersecurity competition hosted by CyberZee, the cybersecurity club of SRM University, Delhi-NCR, Sonepat. Part of the annual technical fest Verge 2026, RAD 2.0 challenges participants to push limits across web exploitation, cryptography, forensics, OSINT, and reverse engineering.

🌌
The Theme

Inspired by Interstellar — traversing the unknown, bending the rules of the digital cosmos, and finding signal in the noise. This isn't just a CTF. It's an expedition into the depths of cyberspace. Do you have the clearance to go beyond?

⚡
Why Participate?

Compete against teams from across the country. Build real-world cybersecurity skills, earn recognition, and connect with a growing community of ethical hackers and security professionals. All skill levels welcome — from first-timers to seasoned CTF players.

🏆
Prizes & Recognition

Top teams win cash prizes, certificates, and trophies. All participants receive a participation certificate. Special awards for best writeups and first blood. Winners get featured on the CyberZee hall of fame and gain direct visibility within our partner network.

Protocols & Clearance

Rules & Eligibility

Eligibility
  • Open to all students — UG, PG, and school level.
  • Participants from any institution across India can compete.
  • Teams of 1–4 members allowed.
  • No prior CTF experience required — beginners welcome.
  • Registration through Unstop only (link in nav & CTA).
Competition Rules
  • Flag format will be disclosed at start — do not brute-force the flag submission system.
  • No sharing of flags, solutions, or writeups during the active window.
  • Attacking the CTFd infrastructure or other teams is strictly prohibited.
  • Use of automated scanners against the platform is banned.
  • Decisions of the CyberZee organizing team are final.
  • Violations result in immediate disqualification.
